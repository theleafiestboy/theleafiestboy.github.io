Useful Resources

Following is a list of useful sources and links encompassing a diverse range of topics that I've stumbled across foraging through the internet. For free-to-read material, you'll find links to the relevant destination. For copyrighted material, I've put in full citations, and I trust that people will be able to 'procure' them from elsewhere :D I'm making this mostly because it's cumbersome to sync all resources across devices, accounts, and sites; and it's equally cumbersome to share resources to different sets of potentially interested people through different channels of communication. The material here includes books/articles, reddit posts, interesting playlists, and a bunch of other stuff. Since a big part of why I made this page was for my own convenience, I sometimes use my own abbreviations and references without full explanation. It goes without saying that I take no responsibility for any content on the links you'll be directed to.

Books, articles, and other print media

Eco-Evo

  • Rice, Sean H.. Evolutionary Theory: Mathematical and Conceptual Foundations. United States, Sinauer, 2004. A classic.
  • Otto, Sarah P., and Day, Troy. A Biologist's Guide to Mathematical Modeling in Ecology and Evolution. United States, Princeton University Press, 2011. Another classic.
  • Mangel, Marc. The Theoretical Biologist's Toolbox: Quantitative Methods for Ecology and Evolutionary Biology. N.p., Cambridge University Press, 2006. Not as well-known, but excellent overall.

South Asia

  • Shripad Damodar Satvlekar. Sanskrit Swyam Shikshak. India, Rajpal & Sons. - Teaches Sanskrit through Modern Standard Hindi first by immersion, then conventionally through grammar.

YouTube Channels and Playlists

  • A Start In Punjabi - Playlist on self-learning Punjabi by ACTDPL, Punjabi University, Patiala.
  • BIC - Bangalore International Centre's channel. Mostly talks by academics.
  • Dissections - Biologybyme - Animal dissections.
  • Black Country Living Museum - Their official channel.
  • British Pathé - Archival footage.
  • Chhayabani - Some 20th century Bangla films.
  • CRW 231: Poetry & What's at Stake - Nicely explained videos on metre in English poetry.
  • Every Frame a Painting - From their channel description, "Every Frame a Painting was a series of video essays about film form, made from April 2014 to September 2016, by Taylor Ramos and Tony Zhou."
  • EVNautilus - Live streams of the deep ocean.
  • EvoEcoSeminars - The name says it all :D
  • Farhan Amin - Rare videos of Hindustani Classical performances.
  • For the Allure of Physics - From their channel description, "An unofficial channel dedicated to Dr. Walter Lewin's recorded MIT OCW physics lectures, complete with descriptions and subtitles."
  • Harvard Natural Sciences Lecture Demonstrations - Useful for science eduction.
